# Project North Star Content Strategy

## Content purpose

Content should establish trust, demonstrate artistic authority, and reveal a
larger philosophy without overexplaining. The voice is confident, warm,
experienced, and precise.

## Primary audiences

- portrait and editorial clients
- serious photographers
- professional models, makeup artists, stylists, and creative collaborators
- prospective Lone Star Retreat participants
- readers following Project North Star
- future education and mentoring clients

## Content pillars

### Portfolio

Curated collections rather than one large gallery:

- Editorial
- Beauty
- Glamour
- Portraiture
- Publications
- Behind the Scenes, when a strong collection exists

Selection should prioritize consistency, craft, trust, and artistic point of
view over volume.

### Private Client

Private Client content should describe luxury portrait, glamour, boudoir, and
commissioned experiences through preparation, trust, comfort, collaboration,
and confidence. Until booking infrastructure is approved, calls to action must
lead to honest direct correspondence rather than imply online availability.

### Experiences

An expandable umbrella for:

- Lone Star Retreat
- future master classes
- private mentoring
- special creative events

Experience content must clearly communicate professionalism, safety, respect,
creative ambition, logistics, and who the experience is for.

### Workshops & Education

Education content may preview workshops, private mentoring, and focused
creative intensives. Program dates, enrollment, pricing, and availability must
remain explicitly forthcoming until those offerings and workflows are
approved.

### Project North Star

The philosophical heart of the platform, expressed through thoughtful journal
entries about:

- reinvention
- creativity
- discipline
- leadership
- photography
- building Lone Star Retreat
- personal growth

It is not positioned as a service.

### About

Lead with Tim’s current creative identity. Aviation supports the story by
showing the discipline, preparation, and leadership that shape his creative
work.

## Voice standards

- Write with clarity and restraint.
- Prefer specific conviction over promotional superlatives.
- Avoid generic luxury language, photography clichés, and inflated claims.
- Keep paragraphs concise and editorial.
- Use first person when personal reflection creates intimacy.
- Use third person where institutional clarity is more useful.

## Image standards

- Final public imagery should be Tim Bennett’s own work.
- Every asset should include title, collection, alt text, caption, credits,
  rights status, and focal-point metadata.
- Image order should tell a story rather than simply rank favorites.
- Behind-the-scenes imagery should reinforce professionalism and process.

## Local content and future CMS

Editable content currently lives in `src/data`. This is a deliberate adapter
layer for future CMS content. When Supabase is connected, preserve the same
content shapes and replace local imports with repository/query functions.

Avoid embedding owner-editable prose, contact details, navigation, image paths,
or collection definitions directly inside route files.

## Publishing workflow

1. Draft
2. Internal review
3. Desktop and mobile preview
4. Accessibility and metadata review
5. Schedule or publish
6. Revisit performance and engagement

Quality and coherence matter more than publishing frequency.
