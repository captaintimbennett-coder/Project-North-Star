# Project North Star Visual Language

## Purpose

This document describes the intended atmosphere, visual choices, and restraint
for the next phase of Project North Star refinement. It complements
`docs/design-system.md`, which remains the implementation contract for tokens,
components, layout rules, accessibility, and responsive behavior.

## Visual principles

The visual language should express Tim Bennett as the founder and visionary of
a connected creative world—not only as an individual photographer. His
artistry remains the visual proof, while his leadership, teaching, mentorship,
creative direction, and experience-building establish the platform's breadth.
Project North Star supplies the discipline, purpose, pacing, and philosophy
underneath that expression. Future visual decisions should not allow the
philosophy to eclipse the person or reduce the platform to a portfolio.

Favor:

- architectural layouts
- generous whitespace
- editorial typography
- cinematic photography
- restrained motion
- subtle luxury
- carefully controlled contrast
- intentional pacing
- moments of quiet
- moments of emphasis

Avoid:

- generic startup aesthetics
- excessive gradients
- oversized shadows
- flashy animations
- visual clutter
- unnecessary decoration

## Color philosophy

Dark tones should create atmosphere. They should not overwhelm the whole
experience or turn the product into a uniformly dark website.

Light backgrounds should provide balance, pacing, and readability.

Gold should function as an accent rather than a dominant color. Bronze and
charcoal should support the overall palette.

The site should alternate between light and dark sections to create visual
rhythm.

## Rhythm and pacing

Rhythm is part of the brand. Luxury is communicated through composition and
pacing as much as color and typography.

Future visual refinement should pay close attention to:

- visual rhythm
- transitions
- moments of quiet
- moments of emphasis
- contrast between light and dark
- sequencing of photography and content

Continue to favor restraint over decoration, clarity over complexity, and
photography as the lead experience.

## Typography

Typography should become more editorial and luxurious while maintaining
excellent readability.

Display typography should communicate refinement rather than technology. Body
typography should remain highly legible and calm.

Any future type changes should be made through the shared design system rather
than one-off page styles.

## Photography

Photography remains the hero of the experience. The interface should support
the imagery rather than compete with it.

Images should have room to breathe. Crops, focal points, and sequencing should
be chosen for emotional tone, composition, and clarity across desktop, tablet,
and compact widths.

## Motion

Motion should remain restrained. It should clarify hierarchy, state, and
navigation rather than decorate the experience.

## Reference assets

Reference assets belong in `docs/brand/references/`. Each asset must be
documented in `docs/brand/references/README.md` with a short explanation of why
it exists and what it should influence.

Expected reference material includes:

- the original Project North Star mood board
- the Project North Star logo
- additional visual inspiration as needed

These assets are reference material, not templates to duplicate. Use them to
understand atmosphere, color relationships, typography, composition, and overall
emotional tone.

Before significant visual design work begins, review the design philosophy, this
visual-language document, and every documented asset in the reference library.
